TAMID Keyrus Data Science Group

"Giving college students the skills and the opportunity to make a difference with data science on a worldwide scale"
Overview

TAMID-Keyrus Data Science Group offers students with opportunity to learn about data science skills, applying those skills to gain professional, enterprise, and real-world experience with data science, while simultaneously serving the public (and assisting private enterprises) to shine light on the underlying truth and make the best decisions.

TAMID-Keyrus Data Science Group revolves around two critical pillars–education curriculum and ventures. Education curriculum is comprised of 4 to 6 weeks of training on the fundamentals of data science, with weekly meetings, where all members learn, explore, and apply a novel data science technique or a model. Ventures, i.e. case projects, are data science based case projects, e.g. application of data science models to a business model, for startups, nonprofits, or private enterprises. Ventures will offer professional data science experience. This standardized structure of TAMID–Keyrus Data Science Group allow for massive scalability for all college campuses.

As the name suggests, TAMID-Keyrus Data Science Group has officially partnered with Keyrus, a management consulting firm specializing in data intelligence and digital experience. With the help from Razvan Nistor, Head of Data Science at Keyrus and Gwendolyn Fernandez, Data Analyst at Keyrus, we were able to develop TAMID-Keyrus Data Science Group's education curriculum.

History

TAMID-Keyrus Data Science Group's idea was first conceived sometime during the summer of 2017, when the founder, Barry Eom, saw the lack of opportunities for college students to explore data science at Tufts and most other college campuses. Why not just create a data science group that can be expanded nationally to other college campuses?

Education

TAMID-Keyrus Data Science Group welcomes anyone from any background to join its prestigious data science group. Members with no coding or data science experience is given a bootcamp on calling libraries and opening files. From there, members are exposed to different data science topic/technique/model on a every other week schedule. Most importantly, members are given the opportunity to apply the data science techniques, constantly developing as an emerging data scientists.

Listed below are the topics that will be covered in the data science education curriculum:

Decision Tree Learning,
Boosted Trees,
Random Forest,
SVM,
Logstic Regression,
Linear Discriminant Analysis,
Neural Networks,
Clustering (K-means, hierarchical, HMMs, etc),
Collaborative filtering (kNN),
anomaly detection via Bayes Classification,
Dimensionality Reduction (PCA and factor analysis),
Reinforcement learning,
Natural Language Processing,
etc

Projects

For now, as TAMID-Keyrus Data Science Group has just launched, we are concentrating on training our members through the education curriculum. From 2019, however, we will be working with multi-billion dollar corporations and startups, namely Vistaprint, DataRobot, and others on data pertaining projects.
